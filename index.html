THE CHALLENGE

Calling all Detroit area designers, developers, civic hackers, and web professionals, announcing the first ever Apps for Detroit Challenge. Join Detroit-area web developers or designers to conceive and build a civic app for your city.

  • Come to the kick-off event on June 28th to learn more and meet a community of web-friendly Detroiters
  • Form a team or work as an individual
  • Familiarize yourself with posted challenges
  • Build an app over two weeks to address these issues or any issue you feel is important in Detroit.
  • Submit by midnight on July 15th!
  • Winners will be announced August 1st.
  • Take home AWESOME prizes!

Representatives from City Hall, along with WDET, Knight Foundation, Michigan Community Resources , CDAD, Data Driven Detroit, and Detroit Works will have an opportunity to share "challenges" or problems to solve with digital apps. Over two weeks, web professionals are invited applications to solve these challenges judged by creativity, impact and civic focus.

RULES

1. Create a working web application to address one of the challenges by area partner institutions.

2. We believe in reuse for public good. Feel free to bring an app from another city to Detroit for the first time — check out the Code for America Brigade apps for inspiration.

3. We ask (but aren't requiring) that your apps be submitted with open licenses: a. Software submitted must be licensed by an OSI compatible license found at http://www.opensource.org/licenses/. b. Design submitted must use the Creative Commons "by attribution" license (link to http://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/2.0/)

What is Apps for Detroit?

What's a civic app?

Good question. We see three models for startups that'd fall within the civic space: 1) they provide services on top of open government data; 2) they bring modern web technologies directly to governments; 3) they change the way citizens ask, get, or need services from government.

What is Code for America?

Founded by Jennifer Pahlka in 2009, Code for America is a non-profit that's trying to make government work more like the internet: more open, more efficient, and more collaborative. Code for America launched with the CfA Fellowship, an annual program, which pairs passionate technologists with innovative city governments to build new solutions to civic problems. (To learn more about the broader context of CfA's work, see Jen's recent TED Talk.)

The CfA Accelerator is an outgrowth of our learnings from the fellowship; primarily a realization that while change from the inside of government is necessary, it must come from the outside as well.
